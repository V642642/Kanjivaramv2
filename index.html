The Beginning

A Weaver's Dream Becomes Legacy

Krishnamurthy Pillai grew up watching his grandmother weave. He saw how the art was fading as synthetic fabrics took over. In 1987, with two looms and four weavers, he started kanjeevaram in a single room in Kanchipuram.

The name "kanjeevaram" comes from the Tamil word meaning "to embrace warmly" — because each saree is meant to embrace the woman who wears it with grace, tradition, and timeless beauty.

Today, his daughter Meera Pillai leads the brand with the same devotion — ensuring every thread meets their exacting standards of authenticity.

What We Stand For

🌿
Sustainability
We use natural dyes and mulberry silk farmed within 100km of our workshop, minimizing our ecological footprint.
🤝
Fair Wages
Every weaver earns 3x the regional average wage. We believe the artisan's prosperity is inseparable from the saree's beauty.
🏅
Authenticity
Every kanjeevaram saree comes with Silk Mark certification and a hand-signed card from the weaver who created it.

Heritage
